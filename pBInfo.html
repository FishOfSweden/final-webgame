The Facts

~ The polar bear is an endangered species.
~ Polar bears need to eat around 12,325 calories per day according to a recent study, 60% more than what scientists had previously thought.
~ Sea ice is shrinking 14% per decade due to climate change, causing major changes in the Arctic and affects the eating patterns of polar bears.
~ There has been a decrease of 770,000 square miles of sea ice since the median measurement in data from 1981-2010.
~ It is predicted that by 2050, the population of bears will be reduced to 10,000, two-thirds less than now. At the current rate, they are expected to go extinct in approximately 2100.
~ There has been a 40% decrease in polar bear population in the last 10 years in the Beaufort Sea region.
~ The population in the Beaufort Sea region deminished from 1,500 to 900 bears. "This is a clear warning sign of the impact a warming Arctic has on ice-dependent species like the polar bear," stated Dr. Pete Ewins, WWF Senior Species Officer in Canada.

How You Can Help

Political Action:

~ Vote for representatives who support focusing on new, ecologically sustainable energy methods and reducing carbon emissions.

Individual Action:

~ Create and/or join organizations that spread awareness about how humans can make lifestyle changes that can help the planet.
~ Participate in initiatives for planting trees and recycling efforts.
~ Avoid driving a car whenever possible - try biking, walking, roller skating, taking the bus, etc. If a car is absolutely necessary, try driving a fuel efficient or electric car if it is financially possible.
~ If driving, avoid idling as much as possible.

Infastructure and Utilities:

~ Insulate buildings efficiently to avoid unnecessary energy waste.
~ Use wind and solar power.
~ Always turn off appliances that are not running and use energy-efficient appliances when possible.
~ Use LED light bulbs.

Purchasing and Consuming:

~ Don't purchase foods and products that will not be used.
~ Avoid consuming meat whenever possible.
~ Buy goods (especially meats) from local farmers and fishermen instead of big business.
~ Purchase products with minimal packaging.

Although indirect, all efforts MATTER! Please contribute in any way to help save the polar bears that is financially reasonable for you.
